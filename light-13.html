Notifying the DVLA That You've Sold Your Vehicle

Before your vehicle is collected, we'd like to highlight some important points regarding the DVLA to ensure a smooth sale.

When you sell your vehicle through Carsmart, it's classified as a 'trade sale' by the DVLA. Transferring the V5C (vehicle logbook) to a dealer/ Carsmart buyer is slightly different from a private sale, so please read the following carefully.

Please do not transfer your V5C, or cancel your vehicle's insurance or tax, until after your vehicle has been collected and payment has been completed. Be sure to note down your document reference number before collection, as you'll need this to complete the transfer.

It's crucial to select the correct option:

Notify the DVLA about the sale of your vehicle after completion. It's the registered keeper's legal duty to inform the DVLA, so we recommend handling this yourself rather than leaving it to the dealer.

You can notify the DVLA that you've sold the vehicle to a dealer on their website by clicking here.

It's crucial to select the correct option:

The online form will ask: "Did you sell the vehicle privately, or to a motor trader?"

  • Make sure you select "Sold it to a motor trader" — this will notify the DVLA that the vehicle is 'in the trade', and your purchasing dealer will not be added as a registered keeper. The sale is then complete!
  • Do not select "Sold it privately to a person or business" — choosing this option will add your purchasing dealer as a registered keeper, devaluing the vehicle and invalidating the V5C needed to sell the car on.

By following these steps, you'll ensure a seamless transfer and avoid any potential issues with the sale of your vehicle. If you have any questions, feel free to reach out—we're here to help!
